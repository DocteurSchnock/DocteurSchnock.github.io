Clarté Loupe

Last updated: July 23, 2026

In one sentence

Clarté Loupe does not collect, store or transmit any personal data; only the advertising component (AppLovin) processes data, with your consent.

The camera

Clarté Loupe uses your phone's camera solely to display, on screen and in real time, a magnified image of what you are looking at. Nothing is recorded, nothing is sent. A "frozen" image stays in the device's memory and disappears as soon as you resume the camera or leave the app. The app works entirely offline.

What we do not do

  • No user account, no sign-up.
  • No personal data collected by the app.
  • No third-party analytics tools.
  • No notifications.
  • No selling of data — we have none.

Advertising (AppLovin)

The app displays a single small ad banner, provided by AppLovin. To do so, AppLovin's software may process certain technical data: the device's advertising identifier, IP address (approximate location), interactions with the ad, and diagnostic data.

On first launch, a very simple screen asks whether you accept personalized advertising. If you decline, non-personalized ads are shown instead. You can change your mind at any time in the app: Menu → Advertising. You can also reset the advertising identifier in your phone's Google settings.

To learn more: AppLovin's Privacy Policy.

The "Remove ads" purchase

The app offers a one-time purchase (€0.99) that permanently removes advertising. Payment is handled entirely by Google Play: we do not receive, see or store any banking data or payment information. The purchase is tied to your Google account and restored automatically, including after reinstalling.

Permissions requested

  • Camera: essential to the magnifier function. It is the only permission the app requests.

Children

The app is intended for an adult audience. It collects no data, from children or adults.
